Atlantis The Palm Pool

Palm Jumeirah, Dubai, United Arab Emirates | Reviewed March 2025

Source: Unsplash

First Impressions

Atlantis The Palm isn't just a hotel—it's a destination. And the pool area reflects that scale. When I first walked out to the pool deck, my immediate thought was: "This is massive." The pool complex spans multiple levels, with different areas for different moods and activities. It's not one pool—it's an aquatic playground.

What struck me most was how well-designed it is for families. There are shallow areas perfect for children, deeper sections for serious swimming, and quiet corners for those seeking peace. The entire space feels like a resort within a resort, with palm trees providing natural shade and the architecture creating a sense of being in a tropical paradise.

The Pool Experience

Multiple Pool Areas

The main pool is enormous—I'd estimate it's at least 50 meters long. But what makes it special isn't just the size. The pool has different zones: a shallow area with built-in seating, a deeper section for swimming laps, and a connecting area that leads to the underwater tunnel views.

I spent time in each area during my visits. The shallow section is perfect for lounging with a drink while still being in the water. The deeper section actually allows for real swimming, which is rare in resort pools. Most are too shallow or too crowded for actual exercise.

The Underwater Tunnel Experience

This is what sets Atlantis apart from every other pool I've visited. Parts of the pool have underwater viewing areas where you can see into the marine habitats. Swimming near these areas and looking down at the marine life below creates an almost surreal experience.

I'm not exaggerating when I say I spent 30 minutes just floating above one of these viewing areas, watching rays and fish swim beneath me. It's like snorkeling without getting your face wet. For families with children, this is absolutely magical. The kids were fascinated, and honestly, so was I.

Water Features & Design

The pool incorporates several water features—gentle waterfalls, fountains, and bubbling areas. These aren't just decorative; they create different acoustic experiences. Near the waterfalls, the sound masks other noise, creating pockets of privacy even in a busy resort setting.

The pool deck is made from a material that stays cool even in direct sunlight, which is essential in Dubai's heat. The design includes plenty of shaded areas, both natural (from palm trees) and constructed (from cabanas and umbrellas).

Service & Amenities

The service here is efficient and friendly, though it operates on a different scale than the more intimate luxury pools. Staff members are constantly circulating, clearing empty glasses, offering fresh towels, and checking if guests need anything.

The poolside dining options are extensive. I tried several items:

  • Poolside burgers: These aren't your typical pool burgers. Made with premium ingredients and served with hand-cut fries, they're actually worth the resort pricing.
  • Fresh salads: The Mediterranean salad was particularly good—fresh, crisp, and perfectly dressed. Great for a lighter option in the heat.
  • Signature smoothies: The "Atlantis Cooler" is a blend of tropical fruits that's both refreshing and filling. Perfect for a mid-afternoon energy boost.
  • Ice cream selection: They have a dedicated ice cream cart that makes rounds throughout the day. The selection changes, but I tried a mango sorbet that was incredible.

The cabanas are spacious and well-equipped, with comfortable seating, mini-fridges, and privacy screens. They're popular, so booking in advance is recommended, especially during peak season.

Atmosphere & Crowd

This is definitely a family-friendly pool, and the atmosphere reflects that. There's energy and activity, but it's positive energy. Children playing, families enjoying time together, groups of friends relaxing. It's not quiet, but it's not chaotic either.

The crowd varies significantly by time of day. Mornings see more serious swimmers and early risers. Midday is peak family time, with the most activity. Late afternoons and evenings tend to be quieter, with more couples and adults-only groups.

One thing I appreciated: despite being a large resort pool, there are always quiet corners. If you want activity, you can find it. If you want peace, you can find that too. The design accommodates both experiences simultaneously.

What Makes It Special

Beyond the obvious size and underwater features, there are details that make this pool experience unique:

  • The marine life integration: The connection to the marine habitats creates an educational element that's rare in resort pools. It's not just swimming—it's an experience.
  • The scale: The pool is large enough that it never feels crowded, even when busy. There's always space to swim, to lounge, to play.
  • The landscaping: The palm trees and tropical plants create a sense of being in a natural oasis, not just a hotel pool.
  • The multiple levels: The pool area has different elevation levels, creating visual interest and different perspectives of the surrounding area.
  • The proximity to the beach: The pool connects seamlessly to the beach area, allowing you to move between saltwater and freshwater experiences easily.

Practical Information

Access & Pricing

Pool access is typically included with hotel stays. Day passes are available but can be expensive. The value is better if you're staying at the resort, as you get full access to all facilities. For current pricing and packages, check the official Atlantis The Palm website.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (7-9 AM) offers the most peaceful experience with fewer families. Midday (11 AM-2 PM) is peak time but also when the pool is most vibrant. Late afternoon (4-6 PM) provides a good balance of activity and space.

What to Bring

Everything is provided, but I'd recommend bringing water shoes if you plan to explore the underwater viewing areas. Sunscreen is essential, and the resort provides high-quality options, but bringing your preferred brand is always a good idea. For families, bring pool toys—the pool is large enough for them, and they're allowed.

Final Thoughts

Atlantis The Palm pool is perfect for families or groups who want both relaxation and activity. It's not the quiet, intimate experience of some luxury pools, but it offers something different: scale, variety, and that unique underwater connection to marine life.

The pool manages to be both impressive in size and well-designed in detail. Every area feels intentional, every feature serves a purpose. It's a pool that accommodates different moods and preferences simultaneously.

If you're looking for a peaceful, adults-only experience, this might not be your first choice. But if you want a pool that offers adventure, relaxation, family fun, and unique features all in one place, Atlantis delivers. It's the pool I'd recommend to families visiting Dubai, and it's earned its place as number two on my list.

The combination of size, features, service, and that incredible underwater element creates an experience that's hard to replicate. It's not just swimming—it's an aquatic adventure.
