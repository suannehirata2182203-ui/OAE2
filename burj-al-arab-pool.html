Burj Al Arab Infinity Pool

Dubai, United Arab Emirates | Reviewed March 2025

Source: Unsplash

First Impressions

Walking into the Burj Al Arab pool area feels like stepping into a different dimension. The infinity edge doesn't just overlook the Gulf—it creates an optical illusion that the pool water flows directly into the ocean. I've visited twice now, once in the morning and once at sunset, and both experiences were completely different yet equally mesmerizing.

The first thing that strikes you isn't the pool itself, but the attention to detail. Every lounger is perfectly positioned. Every towel is folded with precision. The staff moves with a quiet efficiency that never feels intrusive. You're not just paying for access to water—you're paying for an experience that's been choreographed to perfection.

The Pool Experience

Water Quality & Temperature

The water is consistently maintained at what feels like the perfect temperature—warm enough to be comfortable immediately, cool enough to be refreshing. I've never experienced that initial shock of cold water here. The pool is cleaned multiple times daily, and you can actually see the difference in water clarity compared to other luxury pools in the city.

During my morning visit, the water was slightly cooler, which was perfect for a refreshing swim. In the evening, it felt warmer, almost like a natural hot spring. The pool depth varies, with a shallow area perfect for lounging and deeper sections for actual swimming.

The Infinity Edge Magic

This is what you're really paying for. The infinity edge isn't just a design feature—it's an experience. When you're in the pool looking out, especially during sunset, the horizon line disappears completely. The blue of the pool, the blue of the sky, and the blue of the Gulf merge into one continuous canvas.

I spent about 20 minutes just floating near the edge, watching the boats pass by in the distance. There's something meditative about it. The sound of the water, the gentle breeze, and that endless view create a sense of peace that's hard to find in Dubai's bustling energy.

Service & Amenities

The service here is what separates a good pool from a legendary one. Staff members appear exactly when you need them and disappear when you don't. I never had to wait for a drink, never had to ask for a fresh towel, and never felt like I was being watched or judged.

The poolside menu is extensive, and I tried several items over my visits:

  • Fresh fruit platter: Served in a carved watermelon bowl, this was almost too beautiful to eat. The fruits were perfectly ripe and chilled.
  • Grilled seafood: The prawns were massive and perfectly cooked. Served with a side of lemon butter that was addictive.
  • Signature cocktails: The "Arabian Sunset" became my go-to. It's a mix of pomegranate, mint, and something they won't reveal. Refreshing without being too sweet.

The cabanas are spacious and well-shaded, with comfortable seating and privacy screens. Each one has a call button for service, though I never needed to use it—the staff was that attentive.

Atmosphere & Crowd

This isn't a party pool. The atmosphere is sophisticated and relaxed. Most guests are couples or small groups, and everyone respects the unspoken rule of keeping noise levels down. I never heard loud music or rowdy conversations during either visit.

The crowd varies by time of day. Mornings attract serious swimmers and early risers. Afternoons see more families with older children. Evenings are predominantly couples and groups of friends looking for a sophisticated experience.

One thing I appreciated: despite being a luxury destination, there's no pretentiousness. People are there to relax and enjoy, not to be seen. It's refreshing in a city where status can sometimes overshadow experience.

What Makes It Special

Beyond the obvious—the infinity edge, the service, the location—there are subtle details that make this pool experience memorable:

  • The changing rooms: These aren't just functional spaces. They're beautifully designed with premium amenities, including hair dryers, body lotions, and even a steam room. I've spent time here just to enjoy the facilities.
  • The pool deck material: The decking is made from a special material that doesn't get hot in the sun. You can walk barefoot comfortably even at midday in summer.
  • The underwater lighting: During evening visits, the pool has subtle underwater lighting that creates a magical effect as the sun sets.
  • The view of the sail: From certain angles in the pool, you can see the iconic sail-shaped structure of the Burj Al Arab itself, creating a perfect photo opportunity.

Practical Information

Access & Pricing

Access to the pool is typically included with certain room packages or can be purchased as a day pass. The pricing is premium, but for a once-in-a-lifetime experience, it's worth it. I recommend booking in advance, especially during peak season (November to March).

For current pricing and availability, check the official Jumeirah Burj Al Arab website.

Best Time to Visit

I've found that early morning (8-10 AM) offers the most peaceful experience with fewer guests. Sunset (5-7 PM) provides the most dramatic views and lighting. Midday can be quite hot, even with the excellent shade options.

What to Bring

Honestly, you don't need much. Towels, sunscreen, and poolside amenities are all provided. I'd recommend bringing a good book or just planning to disconnect and enjoy the moment. Cameras are welcome, and the staff is happy to help with photos.

Final Thoughts

The Burj Al Arab infinity pool isn't just the best pool I've experienced in the UAE—it's one of the best pool experiences I've had anywhere in the world. The combination of the infinity edge view, impeccable service, and attention to detail creates something that goes beyond swimming.

Is it expensive? Absolutely. Is it worth it? For a special occasion or if you're looking for that perfect Dubai luxury experience, absolutely yes. This is the pool you'll remember years later, the one you'll describe to friends, the one that sets the standard for everything else.

I've been to many pools in Dubai, but this one remains at the top of my list. It's not just about the water or the view—it's about how the entire experience makes you feel. And here, you feel like you're floating above the world, completely at peace.
