Emirates Palace Pool

Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates | Reviewed March 2025

Source: Unsplash

First Impressions

Emirates Palace doesn't just have a pool—it has a statement. The moment you approach the pool area, the golden domes of the palace reflect in the water, creating an almost surreal visual. This isn't subtle luxury. This is opulence made manifest, and it's breathtaking.

I visited Emirates Palace during a weekend trip to Abu Dhabi, and I'll be honest: I was skeptical. Sometimes places that look this luxurious in photos feel sterile or pretentious in person. But walking into the pool area, I understood immediately why this place has its reputation. The attention to detail is extraordinary, and the sense of being in a royal retreat is palpable.

The Pool Experience

Architecture & Design

The pool itself is large and beautifully designed, but it's the surrounding architecture that creates the magic. The golden domes of the palace are visible from every angle, and the way they reflect in the pool water changes throughout the day. In the morning, the reflections are crisp and clear. By afternoon, they shimmer with the movement of the water. At sunset, the entire scene glows.

The pool deck is made from premium materials that feel luxurious underfoot. The loungers are spaced generously, giving each guest a sense of privacy and space. The cabanas are more like private rooms—spacious, well-appointed, and positioned for optimal views of both the pool and the palace architecture.

Water Quality & Comfort

The water is maintained at a perfect temperature year-round. During my visit in early spring, it was warm enough to be immediately comfortable but cool enough to be refreshing. The pool is cleaned multiple times daily, and the water clarity is exceptional—you can see every detail of the pool bottom.

The pool has different depth zones, with a shallow area perfect for lounging and deeper sections for swimming. The transition between depths is gradual and natural, making it comfortable for all swimming abilities.

The Gardens & Surroundings

What sets this pool apart is the integration with the palace gardens. The pool area feels like it's part of a larger landscape, not just a hotel amenity. Palm trees provide natural shade, and the manicured gardens create a sense of being in a private estate.

I spent time just walking around the pool perimeter, admiring the landscaping. Every plant seems intentionally placed, every pathway designed for both function and beauty. It's the kind of attention to detail that makes you slow down and appreciate the moment.

Service & Amenities

The service here is what you'd expect from a palace—attentive without being intrusive, professional without being cold. Staff members anticipate needs before you realize you have them. A fresh towel appears just as you're thinking you need one. A drink arrives exactly when you're ready for it.

The poolside menu is extensive and sophisticated. I tried several items:

  • Afternoon tea service: This isn't typical poolside fare. Full afternoon tea service is available, complete with scones, finger sandwiches, and a selection of teas. It's an experience in itself.
  • Grilled seafood platter: The selection changes daily, but during my visit, it included lobster, prawns, and local fish. Everything was perfectly cooked and beautifully presented.
  • Signature cocktails: The "Palace Gold" cocktail is made with gold leaf—yes, actual gold. It's as much a visual experience as a taste experience.
  • Fresh fruit selection: The fruit platters are works of art, arranged beautifully and featuring both local and imported fruits at peak ripeness.

The changing facilities are exceptional. More like a spa than a pool changing room, with premium amenities, steam rooms, and relaxation areas. I actually arrived early just to spend time in these facilities.

Atmosphere & Crowd

This is an adults-oriented pool experience. While families are welcome, the atmosphere is sophisticated and quiet. Most guests are couples or small groups, and there's an unspoken understanding that this is a place for relaxation and refinement.

The crowd is international and well-traveled. People here appreciate the experience and respect the atmosphere. I never heard loud conversations, never saw rowdy behavior, never felt like the space was being disrespected.

During my visit, the pool was busy but never felt crowded. The generous spacing and multiple areas mean that even at capacity, you can find quiet corners. The design creates natural privacy zones, so even in a shared space, you feel like you have your own area.

What Makes It Special

Beyond the obvious luxury, there are details that make this pool experience unforgettable:

  • The golden reflections: The way the palace's golden domes reflect in the pool water creates a visual experience that's unique to this location. It's genuinely beautiful and changes throughout the day.
  • The sense of history: Emirates Palace has hosted royalty and world leaders. There's a sense of being in a place with history and significance, not just a luxury hotel.
  • The gardens integration: The pool feels like part of a larger estate, not just a hotel amenity. The connection to the palace gardens creates a sense of space and luxury.
  • The attention to detail: Every element, from the pool deck material to the placement of loungers, feels intentional and considered. Nothing is accidental.
  • The changing facilities: These are worth mentioning again. The changing rooms and spa facilities are exceptional, adding value to the overall experience.

Practical Information

Access & Pricing

Pool access is typically included with hotel stays. Day passes are available but are premium-priced. The experience justifies the cost, especially if you plan to spend the full day. For current pricing and availability, check the official Emirates Palace website.

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (7-9 AM) offers the most peaceful experience with the best lighting for photos of the golden reflections. Midday provides the most dramatic reflections as the sun moves. Late afternoon (4-6 PM) offers a quieter atmosphere and beautiful sunset views.

What to Bring

Everything is provided at a premium level, but I'd recommend bringing a good book or planning to fully disconnect. This is a place to slow down and appreciate the experience. Cameras are welcome, and the staff can help with photos. The golden reflections make for incredible photography opportunities.

Final Thoughts

Emirates Palace pool is expensive. There's no way around that. But it's also an experience that's hard to replicate. The combination of the golden architecture, the impeccable service, the sophisticated atmosphere, and the attention to detail creates something that goes beyond just swimming.

This is the pool you visit for a special occasion, for a celebration, for when you want to feel like you're experiencing something truly luxurious. It's not the pool you visit every weekend, but it's the pool you remember years later.

I've been to many luxury pools in the UAE, and Emirates Palace stands out for its unique combination of opulence and atmosphere. The golden domes reflecting in the water, the sense of being in a royal retreat, the impeccable service—it all comes together to create an experience that justifies every dirham.

If you're looking for a quiet, sophisticated, adults-oriented pool experience with a sense of history and luxury, Emirates Palace delivers. It's expensive, but it's also exceptional. And sometimes, that's exactly what you need.
